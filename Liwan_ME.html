About This Recipe

Chicken Biryani Recipe Looking for a delicious, comforting Biryani Recipe that is loaded with warming spices, and that you can make at home? Here you go. I've enjoyed many dishes of biryani from local Indian restaurants, but it's always so satisfying to make at home. Biryani is essentially a spiced rice dish layered with meats or vegetables, though there are many types of biryani, with dozens of regional and personal variations. It truly is a diverse dish representing many cultures. This version is one I prefer, similar to a Hyderabadi biryani, layered with flavored rice, marinated chicken, and aromatic saffron, all made in one pot. If you've been afraid of making this dish at home because of the number of ingredients and steps, I've broken it all down to make it as easy as possible. It's a lot like my favorite local place. This Chicken Biryani recipe is a flavorful and aromatic dish combining tender chicken, fragrant basmati rice, and rich spices. Inspired by South Asian traditions, it’s designed to be simple, delicious, and perfect for home cooking.

CHICKEN BIRYANI RECIPE

Ingredients Instructions

Ingredients

For the Chicken & Marinade

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • ¼ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 kilogram boneless chicken thighs or breasts

For the Rice

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water for boiling

For the Crispy Onions

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup vegetable oil (for frying)

Additional Ingredients

  • ½ teaspoon saffron threads
  • 2 tablespoons warm milk or water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or butter
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (or mix of cilantro and mint)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (for serving)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, spices, and chicken. Marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Parboil rice with whole spices until slightly soft but not fully cooked. Drain and set aside.
  3. Fry sliced onions in hot oil until golden brown and crisp. Drain excess oil.
  4. Soak saffron in warm milk or water for a few minutes.
  5. In a heavy pot, partially cook marinated chicken for a few minutes per side.
  6. Layer partially cooked rice over chicken, then top with fried onions and chopped herbs.
  7. Drizzle the saffron mixture and melted ghee or butter on top.
  8. Cover and cook on low heat for 25–30 minutes until the chicken is done and the rice is fluffy.
  9. Fluff the rice gently and serve hot with lemon juice. Enjoy!
